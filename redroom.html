  Subjects overall responded more positively to this treatment. All retained basic motor functions, although subjects 5 & 11 suffered from mild tremors that affected their ability to perform tasks requiring fine muscle control such as painting and typing. The vision of subjects 4 & 8 was also affected; they were unable to distinguish between most colors and were unaware that this was unusual.
  The treatment only seemed to wipe the subjects' memories of the last 2 to 6 weeks. All subjects were able to clearly remember their friends and family and were concerned for their wellbeing. Despite this inconvenience no subject was able to remember being taken to this facility or any of the subsequent experiments and were subdued with the standard procedures.
