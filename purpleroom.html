Shape Sorting Experiment 9: Stimulant 4.12 with Chimpanzees

Testing how stimulant effects ability to accurately sort objects based on color. Gave them a pile of primary and secondary colored blocks. Used classical conditioning to train chimps to organize blocks by color. Interestingly without prompting the chimps arranged the blocks in a color wheel around them. Even more surprisingly 60% of the wheels were accurate, with opposing colors across from eachother.
When the objects were distributed one at a time, at a fixed rate, all chimps were able to accurately sort 70% of them before treatment. After the stimulant was administered their accuracy rose to 85%.
